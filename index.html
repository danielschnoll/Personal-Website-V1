About Me

My name is Daniel Schnoll, and I am a Sophomore Computer Science student at Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute. I began programming in my senior year in high school with Java in AP Computer Science. In my first semester in college, I took a class in Python, and most recently, I learned C++ in my Data Structures class. At my current internship, have been learning full stack with Django and JavaScript/React.JS. I am still unsure where I want to go in terms of a career, and my interests are in the broad range of full stack, mobile app development, and cyber security.

In the Summer of 2017, I secured a Software Engineering internship at a tech startup, Hickory Training, in NYC. During my internship, I gained valuable experience with Git, PostgreSQL, and full stack development with Django and React.JS. I also handled many customer support tickets, which helped me learn how to professionally and promptly help deal with the support aspect of a startup.

On campus, I am the Vice President of the Troy Consulting Club and the secretary of the Rubik's Cube Club, and consider myself an avid speed-cuber. I am also a member of the Computer Science Club, and I participated in my first hackathon, HackRPI, in November 2016, and I intend on participating in the campus open source club, RCOS (Rensselaer Center for Open Source Software), and rushing a Fraternity. When I am not doing school work or hanging out with my friends, I can usually be found taking Tae Kwon Do classes, playing golf, or reading. I am actually a 3rd Dan black belt, and have been involved with Tae Kwon Do for 14 years.

Currently, I am seeking either an internship for the summer of 2018, or an undergraduate research position. Feel free to download my resume, and if you have any questions, you can contact me at *schnod@rpi.edu*. Thank you!
